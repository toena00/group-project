How its made

Chocolate has a very long proces for the making.

  • Harvesting

    Planting and growing cacao tree seeds and saplings (5-7 years) Harvesting cacao pods (varies, but occurs over the course of a year) Fermenting cocoa beans (3-8 days) Drying beans (5-10 days)

  • Transporting

    Packing and Transporting beans (2-3 weeks) Sorting beans (1-2 hours, depending on how big a batch)

  • Roasting,cracking and grinding

    Roasting beans (20-45 minutes) Cracking beans and Winnowing shells (15-60 minutes) Grinding and refining cocoa nibs (18-24 hours)

  • Tempering

    Resting and aging chocolate (1 day to several weeks) Tempering and molding chocolate bars (1 hour to several hours, depending on scale)

  • Enjoy

Milk Chocolate

The tasty basic chocolate.

Milk chocolate is a form of solid chocolate containing cocoa, sugar and milk. It is the most consumed type of chocolate, and is used in a wide diversity of bars, tablets and other confectionery products.

Dark chocolate

Our famous healthy chocololate.

Dark chocolate is a form of chocolate made of cocoa solids, cocoa butter and sugar. Dark chocolate has a higher cocoa percentage than white chocolate, milk chocolate, and semisweet chocolate.

White chocolate

White chocolate is a chocolate made of cocoa butter, sugar and milk.

White chocolate is a form of chocolate made of cocoa butter, sugar and milk. Unlike milk and dark chocolate, it does not contain cocoa solids, which darken the chocolate.

Ruby chocolate

Ruby chocolate is a chocolate that is pink.

Ruby chocolate is a style or distinct variety of chocolate that is pink or purple in colour.

Pistachio chocolate

Pistachio chocolate is a chocolate made of knafeh and pistachio.

Pistachio chocolate is a chocolate bar with a filling made of knafeh and pistachio.

Almond chocolate

Chocolate-covered almonds are a great option!

Chocolate-covered almonds are a great option! Almonds and chocolate have health benefits such as lowering blood sugar, blood pressure, cholesterol and lowering hunger levels for people who consume them.
